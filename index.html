Abstract

Artificial intelligence (AI) is increasingly entering in-person classrooms as a visible participant in teaching activities, yet we still lack a systematic language for describing how teachers, students, and AI systems interact through multimodal classroom practice. This paper presents APEX, a video-grounded interaction graph for characterizing multimodal teacher–student–AI orchestration in AI-enhanced classrooms. We analyzed 62 exemplary AI-integrated classroom videos from the Sokrates platform and coded 10,552 multimodal interaction events involving teacher behaviors, student participation, and AI/technology outputs. Using K-Means clustering, transition-matrix-based sequence analysis, and descriptive analysis of AI-initiated events, we identified three recurring orchestration patterns: AI-mediated structured teaching, teacher lecturing with a digital whiteboard, and teacher-centered dynamic demonstration. Our findings reveal a "demonstration loop" in which 74% of the cases remained organized around a T-Tech-T pathway, where AI outputs returned almost immediately to teacher control. In contrast, only the AI-mediated pattern exhibited a more interactive T-S-Tech pathway, with AI functioning as a multimodal collaborator. APEX contributes a reusable coding framework and living online artifact for tracking how AI is enacted in frontline classroom practice and for informing future classroom AI design.
